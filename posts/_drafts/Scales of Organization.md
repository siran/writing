## The operator is not about light

The wave equation

$$
\partial_t^2 \Psi - v^2\,\nabla^2 \Psi = 0
$$

appears at every scale of physical organization. In source-free Maxwell
transport, $v = c$ and $\Psi$ is the field itself. In acoustics, $v = v_s \ll
c$ and $\Psi$ is the pressure perturbation of a bulk medium. In gravitational
wave propagation, $v \approx c$ and $\Psi$ is the organized perturbation of
aggregate mass distribution.

The operator is the same. The speed changes. The reason is the same each time:
organized units at one scale form the medium for the next, and the speed of
propagation through that medium is

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{K}{\rho}},
$$

where $K$ is the coupling stiffness between neighboring units and $\rho$ is
their inertia. Both $K$ and $\rho$ are inherited from the electromagnetic
structure of the units themselves. No new law is introduced at any scale.

The question is not why the wave equation appears. It is what changes between
scales.


## What changes between scales

At each scale there is a characteristic frequency $\omega_0$ — the lowest
internal mode of the organized units at that scale. This frequency sets the
boundary between regimes:

- **Below $\omega_0$**: the units respond as rigid bodies. The medium is a
  continuum. The wave equation holds with speed $v = \sqrt{K/\rho}$.
- **Near $\omega_0$**: the drive couples into the internal structure of the
  units. Discrete modes are excited. The continuum description breaks down.
- **Above $\omega_0$**: partial internal excitations are driven that do not
  re-close into stable modes. These radiate downward. This is dissipation.

Every scale transition is governed by the same logic: below $\omega_0$ of the
units at that scale, the wave equation of that scale holds cleanly. At
$\omega_0$, you cross into the regime of the units themselves.


## The hierarchy

**Electromagnetic — $v = c$**

Source-free Maxwell transport. The wave equation acts on the field directly.
No medium. The speed $c$ is set by the transport law. This is the fundamental
regime: no scale below it.

**Quantum / toroid-internal — $\omega \sim \omega_0$**

At wavelengths comparable to the size of a self-sustaining toroidal closure,
the field resolves the internal structure of the mode. The wave equation no
longer describes a continuum — it describes the discrete standing-wave
frequencies of the closure itself. This is the Schrödinger regime: a
narrow-band approximation of the standing wave of energy flow, acting on the
envelope of the mode. The speed is still approximately $c$ locally, but the
organized mode has an effective group velocity set by its winding structure.

**Acoustic — $v_s \ll c$**

At wavelengths large compared to the toroid spacing $d_0$, the closures are a
continuum medium. Perturbations propagate as density waves with

$$
v_s = \sqrt{\frac{K}{\rho}},
\qquad
K \sim \frac{Q^2}{d_0^3},
\quad
\rho = \frac{nE}{c^2}.
$$

Both $K$ (from charge coupling) and $\rho$ (from trapped energy) are
electromagnetic quantities. The speed $v_s \ll c$ is forced by the stability
of the medium: inter-toroid coupling is much weaker than toroid self-energy.

**Elastic / solid-state**

The same acoustic operator, but with different $K$. In a crystal lattice,
toroids are more tightly bound and anisotropically arranged. The stiffness
tensor replaces the scalar $K$, giving multiple wave types — longitudinal and
transverse — with different speeds and polarizations. The operator is still
$\partial_t^2 - v^2\nabla^2$ in each polarization channel. The regime boundary
is still $\omega_0$ (the Debye frequency of the lattice), above which the
discrete lattice structure is resolved.

**Gravitational — $v \approx c$**

At the largest scales, the $(m,n)$ organization of individual toroidal closures
averages out across an aggregate of many randomly-oriented toroids. What remains
is the scalar energy $NE/c^2$ distributed over shells. Perturbations in this
aggregate mass distribution propagate as organized disturbances in the
shell-distributed energy. The propagation speed returns to $\approx c$ — because
gravity in this framework is refraction of one electromagnetic flow by another's
organized load, and refraction inherits the transport speed.


## The symmetry

$$
v_\text{EM} = c \quad\longrightarrow\quad v_s \ll c \quad\longrightarrow\quad v_\text{grav} \approx c
$$

The hierarchy begins and ends at $c$. This is not accidental. The two regimes
that operate at $c$ are both transport-level: electromagnetic transport (the
fundamental) and gravitational propagation (refraction of one transport by
another). The acoustic valley in between is the regime where organized structure
— the toroidal closures — provides inertia that slows the wave below $c$. As
soon as the organization averages out (aggregate mass, large scale), the speed
returns to $c$.

The acoustic regime is the one scale where the organized structure of matter
shows up as a slowdown. Below that scale (quantum/EM) and above it (gravity),
transport operates at the fundamental speed.


## Dissipation as downward coupling

At each scale, dissipation is excitation of the scale below:

- **Acoustic dissipation**: a sound perturbation excites partial internal
  circulations of toroidal closures that do not re-close. These radiate as
  electromagnetic transport. Acoustic energy becomes EM energy.
- **Quantum dissipation**: an excited internal mode of a toroidal closure
  decays by emitting an organized perturbation into the EM field — a photon in
  the standard language, here a propagating EM mode.
- **Gravitational dissipation**: a perturbation in aggregate mass distribution
  radiates organized EM and acoustic energy as the aggregate reorganizes.

The direction is always downward: energy cascades from the coarse scale to the
fine scale. This is the arrow of time at the level of scale hierarchy — the
same logic as the entropy argument applied to the organization of transport.


## Coherent limits

At each scale, the dissipation mechanism is absent when all units are
phase-synchronized:

- **Acoustic coherence**: all toroidal closures in the same mode, phases
  aligned. No partial internal excitations possible. Sound propagates without
  dissipation at all frequencies. This is the superfluid.
- **Charge coherence**: toroidal closures with aligned charge structure,
  synchronized in phase. No resistive dissipation of organized flow. This is
  the superconductor.
- **Gravitational coherence**: whether there is a regime in which aggregate
  mass is organized coherently — all toroids phase-synchronized at the
  gravitational scale — is an open question. Cosmologically, this may describe
  the early universe before the organized structure of matter diversified.

In each case, coherence eliminates the coupling mechanism that would transfer
energy to the scale below. The wave propagates as if the lower scale does not
exist.


## What is established and what is not

**Established** (in this framework):
- EM wave equation from source-free Maxwell transport
- Acoustic wave equation from toroidal medium (Ch 11a)
- Dissipation as non-closing internal circulations
- $v_s \ll c$ forced by medium stability
- Superfluid as coherent toroid limit

**Derived in outline, not in detail**:
- Elastic/solid-state as anisotropic acoustic regime
- Gravitational wave speed returning to $c$ via transport-level refraction

**Speculative**:
- The gravitational coherence limit
- Whether the hierarchy is exhaustive or admits further scales above gravity
- The cosmological interpretation of the early-universe coherent limit


## Closing

The wave equation is not the equation of light. It is the equation of organized
energy transport — at any scale, in any medium made of organized units from the
scale below. The speed is the signature of the organization.

Source-free Maxwell transport produces one operator and a hierarchy of regimes.
Each regime is a different answer to the question: what are the organized units,
how strongly do they couple, and how much energy do they carry as inertia?

The answers run from the field itself (EM, $v = c$) through matter (acoustic,
$v_s \ll c$) to the aggregate of matter (gravitational, $v \approx c$). The
hierarchy is not imposed. It is what source-free transport produces when
organized into progressively coarser structures.
