---
title: The Experiment Nobody Did
date: 2026-04-23
---

A dielectric slows light because the medium's polarization response is an
in-phase electromagnetic wave riding alongside the incident one. At a
Mach-Zehnder recombination, the second arm beam *is* exactly that in-phase
wave — same math, same formula, and for equal beams $c_\text{eff} = c/2$.

Standard optics says no: Maxwell in vacuum is linear. But the specific test
that would settle it — isolate a bright fringe, send it into a glass slab at
$\theta_i \approx 50^\circ$, watch for total internal reflection at the
critical angle $\theta_c \approx 48.6^\circ$ — apparently hasn't been done.
A hundred years of interferometry, a binary outcome, a glass slab and a CCD.
