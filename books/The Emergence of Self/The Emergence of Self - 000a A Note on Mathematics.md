# A Note on Mathematics

It is completely safe to ignore any mathematical symbol in this book.

The mathematics is included only to tighten certain claims, mark structure more
precisely, or reassure readers who want an explicit formal skeleton. It is not
the primary carrier of the argument.

If an equation appears opaque, you may read it in the simplest possible way:

- \(x\) usually names the present organization of a loop,
- \(m\) usually names an imprint carried by that loop,
- \(u\) usually names current input from outside,
- and the equations simply say that what happens next depends on some
  combination of those.

Nothing essential in the philosophical or conceptual argument depends on being
able to manipulate the notation. The book can be read in full without doing any
mathematics at all. If you prefer, simply skim past the equations and keep
reading.

The symbols are there for precision, not for exclusion.
