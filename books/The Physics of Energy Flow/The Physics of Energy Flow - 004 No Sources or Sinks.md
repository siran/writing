# 4. No Sources or Sinks

Source-free does not mean the current has zero divergence. It means
that no extra source term is introduced. All local change in energy density is
accounted for by transport. In later local shorthand, this is written:

$$
\partial_t u + \nabla \cdot \mathbf{J} = 0.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{J} = -\partial_t u.
$$

A nonzero divergence of $\mathbf{J}$ therefore does not mean that energy begins
or ends at a point. It means only that a small region has a net inflow or
outflow, and so its stored energy changes.

Source-free means: no primitive creation, no primitive annihilation, no added
term beyond transport itself. The next question is different. It is not whether
the current is divergence-free, but how transport is organized in space.
$\mathbf{J}$ handles the accounting: how much energy crosses a surface, and in
what direction. It does not yet tell us what spatial pattern that transport
takes through empty space.
