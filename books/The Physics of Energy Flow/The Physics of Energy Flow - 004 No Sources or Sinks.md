# 4. No Sources or Sinks

In free space, there are no energy sources or sinks. Energy transported across
a region does not change this fact. It changes only how much energy is stored
there. In later local shorthand, this is written:

$$
\partial_t u + \nabla \cdot \mathbf{S} = 0.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{S} = -\partial_t u.
$$

A nonzero divergence of $\mathbf{S}$ therefore does not mean that energy begins
or ends at a point. It means only that a region has a net inflow or outflow,
and so its stored energy changes.

This book studies that source-free regime throughout. No extra source term is
needed for the reconstruction that follows. The next question is different. It
is not whether the flow balances locally, but what shape the reorganization of
energy takes when understood as a complete process rather than region by region
numerical accounting. $\mathbf{S}$ handles the accounting: how much energy
crosses a surface, and in what direction. It does not yet tell us the full
spatial pattern that transport takes through empty space.

Once the redistribution is recognized as continuous and source-free in empty
space, the possible forms of transport become constrained. The next step is to
see what those constraints are.
