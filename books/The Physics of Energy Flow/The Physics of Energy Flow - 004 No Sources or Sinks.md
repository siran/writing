# 4. No Sources or Sinks

Source-free does not mean the flow has zero divergence. It means
that no extra source term is introduced. All local change in energy density is
accounted for by transport. In later local shorthand, this is written:

$$
\partial_t u + \nabla \cdot \mathbf{S} = 0.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{S} = -\partial_t u.
$$

A nonzero divergence of $\mathbf{S}$ therefore does not mean that energy begins
or ends at a point. It means only that a small region has a net inflow or
outflow, and so its stored energy changes.

Source-free means: no primitive creation, no primitive annihilation, no added
term beyond transport itself. That is the regime studied throughout this book.
Explicit source terms are not needed for the reconstruction that follows. The
next question is different. It is not whether the flow is divergence-free, but
what shape the reorganization of energy takes when understood as a complete
process rather than region by region numerical accounting. $\mathbf{S}$ handles
the accounting: how much energy crosses a surface, and in what direction. It
does not yet tell us the full spatial pattern that transport takes through
empty space.

Once the redistribution is recognized as continuous and source-free in empty
space, the possible forms of transport become constrained. The next step is to
see what those constraints are.
