# 6. Curl Preserves Flow

**Claim.** To preserve the source-free condition on $\mathbf{F}$, the evolution
law must preserve $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} = 0$. Curl does this identically.

Write the local evolution in the form

$$
\partial_t \mathbf{F} = \nabla \times \mathbf{A}[\mathbf{F}],
$$

where $\mathbf{A}[\mathbf{F}]$ is any vector field built from the current flow
configuration. Taking the divergence gives

$$
\partial_t (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F})
=
\nabla \cdot (\nabla \times \mathbf{A}[\mathbf{F}])
=
0.
$$

This is an identity, not a special case. It does not depend on whether
$\mathbf{A}[\mathbf{F}]$ is linear or nonlinear. Curl turns a flow without
creating endpoints.

Curl is therefore the differential form of source-free reorganization:
continuous turning, with no tearing and no start or end points introduced by
the evolution itself.
