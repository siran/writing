# 12. Newton as Flux Accounting

**Claim.** Newton's second law is not a primitive description of how forces
act on particles. It is a statement about momentum flux through the surface
surrounding a localized energy configuration.

The electromagnetic stress-energy tensor $T^{\mu\nu}$ encodes both energy
density and momentum flux. The spatial components $T^{ij}$ describe the rate
at which the $i$-th component of momentum crosses a surface oriented in the
$j$-th direction.

For a closed, stable field configuration — what we call a particle — the
total momentum inside a volume $V$ is:

$$
p_i = \frac{1}{c^2} \int_V S_i \, dV.
$$

Its rate of change is determined entirely by the flux of $T^{ij}$ through
the bounding surface $\partial V$:

$$
\frac{dp_i}{dt} = -\oint_{\partial V} T^{ij} \, dA_j.
$$

The right-hand side is what we call *force*. It is not a primitive causal
agent acting on a particle. It is the net rate at which momentum is being
transferred across the boundary of the region occupied by the configuration.

A mediating "force field" inserted between supposedly independent substrates
does not rescue their independence. If interaction is real, a common
structure is already present, and force is only the bookkeeping of that
coupling.

$F = dp/dt$ is bookkeeping for momentum flux. Nothing more is needed.
Particles are regions; forces are boundary integrals; Newton's law is the
continuity equation applied to momentum.
