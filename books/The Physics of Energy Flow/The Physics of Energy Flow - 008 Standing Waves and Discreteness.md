# 8. Standing Waves and Discreteness

One of the central early quantum facts is that hydrogen does not radiate a
continuous rainbow. It radiates in discrete lines. In the usual story, those
lines are tied to an electron moving between allowed orbits. Here the stronger
claim is different: what is discrete is not a particle path, but the allowed
standing-wave organizations of the field itself.

Fields matter. If matter is a bounded configuration of source-free
electromagnetic energy flow, then the bounded object must be a real standing
wave: a self-consistent closed pattern of Maxwellian transport whose local flow
remains dynamical while the whole configuration persists as one organized mode.

On a closed support, standing waves are fixed by global self-consistency. Along
any closed loop of length $L$, the field must reproduce itself after one full
circuit:

$$
n \lambda = L.
$$

This already forces discreteness. Only certain wavelengths fit the closure. A
torus is the natural support because it permits continuous circulation to close
smoothly on itself along two independent cycles. The bounded standing wave is
therefore labeled by an integer pair $(m,n)$ rather than by a single continuous
parameter.

The Rydberg pattern can then be read as a reorganization of a fixed total
energy into progressively finer standing-wave partitions. If a bounded mode is
subdivided into an $n \times n$ standing-wave partition on the same support,
the characteristic energy per coherent cell scales as

$$
E_n \propto \frac{E_1}{n^2}.
$$

So line differences take the form

$$
\Delta E \propto \frac{1}{m^2} - \frac{1}{n^2},
\qquad m>n.
$$

The integers are not imposed from outside. They are the counting numbers of the
standing-wave closure itself. The ground state is the tightest
self-consistent standing wave allowed by the geometry. Higher levels are finer
reorganizations of the same bounded field.

So discreteness begins as standing-wave closure. The physical object is not a
particle path plus a field; it is the field itself organized into one bounded
mode. Once that standing wave exists, its further global aspects can be
separated: the trapped load of the closed circulation appears as mass in the
next chapter, and the through-hole character of that same toroidal standing
wave appears as charge in the chapter after that.
