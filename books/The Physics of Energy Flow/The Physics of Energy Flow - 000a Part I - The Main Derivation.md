# Part I — The Main Derivation

Part I contains the main chain of the book.

It begins from the minimal ontological claim that something exists and
redistributes continuously. From there it derives continuity, source-free
transport, curl preservation, double-curl or Maxwellian transport, and the
first organized consequences of bounded electromagnetic closure.

The thirteen chapters that follow carry that chain through the first physical
reconstructions: topology and discreteness, mass, charge, the Schrödinger
sector, Newtonian flux accounting, and gravity as refraction.

The companion Part II then gathers what comes after the spine itself:

- corollaries that clarify the ontology,
- and appendices that supply later technical derivations without interrupting
  the main line.
