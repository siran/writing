# 6. Curl Works

**Claim.** Curl-based evolution preserves divergence-free structure
identically — not as a special case, but as an algebraic identity. It is
therefore the minimal valid evolution rule for source-free transport.

Suppose instead:

$$
\partial_t \mathbf{F} = \nabla \times \mathbf{G}.
$$

Taking the divergence of both sides:

$$
\partial_t (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F}) = \nabla \cdot (\nabla \times \mathbf{G}) = 0.
$$

The last equality is not an approximation or a special condition. It is a
vector calculus identity: *the divergence of any curl is exactly zero,
always*. No fine-tuning is required. No constraint on $\mathbf{G}$ is
needed.

This means: if $\mathbf{F}$ is divergence-free at $t = 0$, curl-based
evolution keeps it divergence-free for all time. The constraint propagates
automatically.

Curl-based dynamics is not the only mathematically conceivable evolution
law, but it is the *minimal* one that is consistent with source-free
transport. Gradient dynamics fails. Curl dynamics succeeds. There is no
simpler alternative in three dimensions.
