# 4. Divergence-Free

**Claim.** In regions where energy is neither created nor annihilated, the
flux field itself must be divergence-free.

$$
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{S} = 0.
$$

This is the observational fact that distinguishes empty space from a source.
We do not observe spontaneous point-creation of energy in the vacuum. We do
not observe spontaneous annihilation. In the absence of sources and sinks,
energy flows through — it does not terminate or originate.

The divergence-free condition means that flux lines have no endpoints in
empty space. Every flux line that enters a region must exit it. What enters
one face of a cube must exit another face. Flow circulates; it does not
accumulate or vanish.

This constraint is geometric. It says nothing about time or dynamics. It
characterizes the *shape* of admissible flow patterns in source-free regions
of space — and that shape is one of closed circulation.

This is the constraint that will determine everything else. Once we require
that energy transport be divergence-free, the space of possible dynamics
collapses dramatically.
